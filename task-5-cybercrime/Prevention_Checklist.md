                             Stay Safe Online (Prevention Checklist)
1.	General Digital Safety Tips
•	We should use strong and unique passwords for all accounts. 
•	We must enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible. 
•	Avoid clicking on unknown links or suspicious emails/messages.
•	Secure Your SIM Card: We need to set a SIM PIN on our phone. If our phone is stolen, this prevents the thief from putting our SIM into another device.
•	Always download apps from trusted sources like Play Store or App Store.
•	We should logout from accounts when using shared or public devices.
•	We must not share personal information (Aadhaar, phone number, address) publicly.
•	Public Wi-Fi: We should never log into our bank portal or student academic dashboard using free campus or cafe Wi-Fi without a trusted VPN as it may lead to packet-sniffing attacks.
•	We should regularly check which apps have permission to access your SMS and Contacts. Many malicious "Instant Loan" or "Fake Reward" apps use these permissions to harass us.
•	Before entering our personal sensitive information such as college roll number or password on any portal, we must ensure the URL starts with https:// and look for the padlock icon. 

2.	UPI and Financial Safety Tips
•	Remember: We do not need to enter a PIN to receive money. This is also known as ‘Zero-Pin Rule.’
•	We must not share our UPI PIN, OTP or bank details with anyone, no matter how close they are with us.
•	We should always verify before accepting  the ‘collect requests’ on UPI apps.
Don’t approve ‘Collect Requests’ blindly Reject unknown payment requests. Scammers often send ‘request’ links claiming prize or refund.
•	Do not scan unknown QR codes sent by strangers.
•	We have to double-check the receiver’s name before making any payment.
•	Verify the Display Name: Before going to 'Pay' on apps like GPay, PhonePe, or Paytm, we are needed to double-check the verified name that appears. Scammers often use names like "SBI Customer Care" or "Electricity Board" as their display names to appear official.
•	We must use our bank’s mobile app to set a reasonable daily limit on UPI transactions.
•	We also need to avoid installing screen-sharing apps when asked by unknown callers. 

3.	Cybercrime: Reporting and Awareness
•	In case of any fraud activities with us, we must immediately report online at: National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in).
•	We can also call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 for quick assistance.

4.	Conclusion
             Immediate reporting increases the chances of recovering lost money.
             Being aware and cautious is the best defense against cybercrime in this era of digital age.
