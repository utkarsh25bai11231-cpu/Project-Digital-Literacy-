### Stay Safe Online (Prevention Checklist)

---

## 1. General Digital Safety Tips

- We should use strong and unique passwords for all accounts.  
- We must enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible.  
- Avoid clicking on unknown links or suspicious emails/messages.  
- **Secure Your SIM Card:** Set a SIM PIN on your phone. If your phone is stolen, this prevents misuse of your SIM.  
- Always download apps from trusted sources like Play Store or App Store.  
- We should logout from accounts when using shared or public devices.  
- We must not share personal information (Aadhaar, phone number, address) publicly.  
- **Public Wi-Fi:** Never log into banking portals or academic dashboards using free Wi-Fi without a trusted VPN, as it may                       lead to packet-sniffing attacks.  
- Regularly check which apps have permission to access your SMS and Contacts. Malicious apps may misuse this data.  
- Before entering sensitive information, ensure the URL starts with `https://` and check for the padlock icon.

---

## 2. UPI and Financial Safety Tips

- **Remember:** You do not need to enter a PIN to receive money (*Zero-PIN Rule*).  
- Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank details with anyone.  
- Always verify before accepting **collect requests** on UPI apps.  
- Do not approve unknown payment requests blindly. Scammers often send fake requests claiming prizes or refunds.  
- Do not scan unknown QR codes sent by strangers.  
- Double-check the receiver’s name before making any payment.  
- **Verify Display Name:** Before paying via apps like GPay, PhonePe, or Paytm, confirm the verified name. Scammers may use                              fake official names.  
- Set a reasonable daily limit on UPI transactions using your bank’s app.  
- Avoid installing screen-sharing apps when instructed by unknown callers.

---

## 3. Cybercrime: Reporting and Awareness

- In case of fraud, immediately report at: **National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in)**  
- You can also call the **Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930** for quick assistance.

---

## 4. Conclusion

Immediate reporting increases the chances of recovering lost money.  
Being aware and cautious is the best defense against cybercrime in the digital age.
